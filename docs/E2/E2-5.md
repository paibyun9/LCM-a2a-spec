# @E2-5. Illustrative Application Domains (Non-Exhaustive)
## Examples of Where Deterministic Policy Execution Is Applicable

### Purpose of This Document

This document provides illustrative examples of domains in which LCM’s deterministic, state-based policy execution model may be applicable.

These examples are provided for conceptual understanding only.  
They do not constitute commitments, guarantees, product offerings, or limitations on LCM’s scope.

This document is **non-binding** and does not modify or override the **@E1 Constitution**.

---

### General Principle

LCM is not domain-specific.

Any environment in which outcomes must be derived from predefined rules—without discretion, interpretation, or retroactive adjustment—may be compatible with LCM’s execution model.

If a process can be represented as a flowchart where every decision point is based on objective data, it is a candidate for LCM.

The following examples illustrate this principle across different contexts.

---

### Transactional Policy Enforcement

Systems that manage refunds, cancellations, exchanges, or eligibility decisions based on predefined policies.

In such environments, LCM can be used to apply vendor-defined rules exactly as written, producing outcomes that are consistent, traceable, and reproducible.

---

### Eligibility and Entitlement Determination

Public or private administrative workflows that require objective evaluation of eligibility conditions.

Examples include access qualification, benefit entitlement, or permission gating where discretionary judgment is undesirable or prohibited.

LCM applies rules strictly as defined, without assessing intent, fairness, or exceptional circumstances.

---

### Contractual Lifecycle Alignment

Multi-party systems where contractual terms must remain synchronized with operative policies at the time of execution.

LCM can ensure that actions taken under a contract are evaluated against the policy snapshot that was effective at the relevant moment, preventing post-hoc reinterpretation.

---

### Content and Publication Governance

Systems governing publication, correction, retention, or takedown decisions based on explicit policy criteria.

LCM does not evaluate the substance or merit of content.  
It executes only the rules that define when and how actions are permitted.

---

### Access and State-Based Authorization

Infrastructure or platform environments where access is granted strictly according to predefined states.

LCM can be used to enforce eligibility transitions without discretionary approval, manual review, or implicit negotiation.

---

### Audit, Accountability, and Traceability Frameworks

Frameworks requiring immutable records of how and when policies were applied.

By binding each outcome to a fixed policy snapshot and explicit system state, LCM enables auditability without reconstructing intent or judgment after the fact.

---

### Non-Exhaustive Nature of Examples

The domains listed above are illustrative only.

LCM does not prescribe how it must be used, nor does it limit its applicability to these examples.  
Any system that requires deterministic rule execution without gray zones may fall within the conceptual scope of LCM.

---

This document is provided for explanatory purposes only.

---

### Summary (Non-Authoritative)

LCM is applicable wherever rules must be executed as written.

It is not defined by industry or use case, but by a requirement for deterministic, non-discretionary policy application.
